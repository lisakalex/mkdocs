# Questions

Here’s a compiled list of strong, targeted questions to ask during your interview for the **DevOps Platform Engineer**
role at Stark Software International Limited. These questions are grouped by category to help you demonstrate interest,
assess fit, and gather key info on the role, team, tech, company stability/growth, and day-to-day realities.

They draw from the job ad (e.g., 80% on-prem / 20% cloud, first dedicated Platform Engineer, engineering team of 20+,
heavy on-prem focus, standardization, legacy systems, influence on standards, culture/work-life balance) and the
company's context (energy sector specialist, recent Siemens UK Energy Services acquisition in 2025 completing early
2026, Macquarie investment in 2025 for growth, ~200–500 employees with recent expansion via acquisition).

### About the Role & Responsibilities

1. As the first dedicated Platform Engineer, what are the top 2–3 immediate priorities or pain points you'd expect me to
   tackle in the first 3–6 months (e.g., specific CI/CD standardization, observability setup, or legacy infra
   modernization)?
2. The role is described as 80% on-prem and 20% cloud (AWS/Azure). Can you give examples of the current on-prem
   infrastructure (e.g., data centers, hardware types, self-hosted Kubernetes/OpenShift setup) and how the cloud portion
   is used today?
3. How much direct influence will the Platform Engineer have on engineering standards across the squads? For instance,
   will there be formal processes like architecture reviews, RFCs, or working groups where I'd present recommendations
   and drive adoption?
4. The ad mentions dealing with some legacy systems and slower release cycles. What kinds of legacy elements are most
   common (e.g., older .NET apps, monolithic services, physical hardware dependencies), and how open is the team to
   modernization efforts?
5. Beyond CI/CD pipelines, Terraform, Docker/Kubernetes, and observability, what other day-to-day tasks would take up a
   significant portion of time (e.g., supporting test data refresh, security audits, mentoring, or on-call for platform
   issues)?

### About the Team & Collaboration

6. The engineering team is 20+ people split into 3 squads. How does the Platform Engineer interact with them—e.g.,
   embedded in squads, central service provider, or a mix? Are there any dedicated platform-focused rituals or
   ceremonies?
7. Who would I report to (e.g., the Team Leader from the original job profile, or someone in engineering leadership),
   and how is success measured for this role in the first year (KPIs, objectives, or qualitative feedback)?
8. With the recent acquisition of Siemens' UK Energy Services business (completing early 2026), how has that impacted
   the engineering team—e.g., new people/tools/processes joining, integration challenges, or expanded scope for platform
   work?
9. The ad highlights mentoring and technical leadership. Are there junior engineers or apprentices in the team I’d be
   expected to support, and is there a formal mentoring structure?

### About Technology & Stack

10. Can you walk through the current CI/CD setup (e.g., Azure DevOps vs. GitLab vs. GitHub Actions) and any self-hosted
    runners or on-prem constraints?
11. For containerization/orchestration: Is it primarily self-hosted Kubernetes/OpenShift today? Any plans to increase
    cloud-native usage in the next 12–24 months?
12. What observability tools are in place now (e.g., Prometheus/Grafana, DataDog, Dynatrace), and what gaps exist in
    logging/tracing/alerting that this role would address?
13. How integrated is the Microsoft/.NET stack with the platform work? Are there React frontends or MuleSoft
    integrations that Platform would touch?
14. Given the energy sector focus, are there specific compliance/security requirements (e.g., data sovereignty, GDPR for
    metering data, cyber essentials) that heavily influence platform decisions?

### About Company, Growth & Culture

15. With the Macquarie Capital investment and Siemens acquisition, what does the company's growth roadmap look like over
    the next 2–3 years, particularly for the technology/engineering function?
16. The ad emphasizes work-life balance (no overtime, flexible hours, 8% pension match, private medical, 25–28 days
    holiday + buy 5 more). How does the team actually practice this—e.g., typical working hours, on-call expectations,
    or support for personal commitments?
17. The office is 1 day/week in Horley (near Gatwick). How hybrid/remote-friendly is the rest of the week, and what's
    the typical split for the engineering team?
18. What does success look like for the engineering department as a whole in the next year or two, and how does the
    Platform Engineer contribute to that?
19. Can you share more about the "Tech For Good" aspect—e.g., specific projects where the platform enables carbon
    reduction or Net Zero goals internationally?

### Practical / Closing Questions

20. What is the interview process from here (e.g., technical test, pair-programming, presentation, stakeholder
    interviews)?
21. Are there any upcoming projects or changes (post-acquisition integration) that might affect this role in the short
    term?

These questions show you've done your homework, are thoughtful about the challenges (on-prem heavy, legacy,
first-in-role), and are genuinely interested in impact and fit. Prioritize 8–10 based on the interview flow—start with
role/team ones to build rapport. Good luck; this seems like a role with real ownership in a purposeful, growing company!
If you share more about your background or concerns, I can refine these further.
